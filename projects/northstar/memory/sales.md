# Sales Playbook — Northstar

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary: Solo crypto trader / DeFi power user
- Trades $10k–$500k on Solana DEXes regularly
- Already familiar with Raydium, Orca, Jupiter
- Frustrated by manually watching spreads
- Values transparency and open-source code over black-box products
- Hangs out on Twitter/X, Discord (Solana ecosystem servers), Reddit r/solana

### Secondary: Small trading desk / crypto fund
- 2–10 person team managing $500k–$5M
- Looking to automate parts of their Solana strategy
- Needs reliability, risk controls, and auditability
- Decision maker: head of trading or a quant developer

### Not a fit (yet)
- Total beginners — too much setup required
- Traders on other chains (Ethereum, BSC) — wrong ecosystem
- Anyone expecting "set and forget" passive income

## What We're Selling

Northstar is an open-source Solana DEX arbitrage bot.
Current stage: paper trading (no live funds at risk).

**Value props:**
1. Transparent — all code public, all results published
2. Safe — strict risk guardrails built in (circuit breakers, max position size)
3. Proven process — paper trade first, live only when profitable
4. Educational — learn how DEX arbitrage actually works

**Offer tiers (to be defined when live trading launches):**
- Free tier: open-source, self-hosted, community support
- Pro tier: hosted version, private Discord, priority support (price TBD)
- Fund tier: white-glove setup + custom risk parameters (price TBD)

## Objection Handling
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Paper trading isn't real" | "Exactly right — that's why we do it first. We'll only go live once we have 30+ days of consistent paper profit. You can watch every trade in real time." |
| "Why open source? Anyone can copy it" | "Arbitrage is a game of execution speed and reliability, not secrecy. Our edge is the system, not the secret." |
| "How do I know it won't blow up my account?" | "Three circuit breakers: daily loss limit (3%), all-time drawdown limit (10%), max trade size ($500). It stops itself before it causes serious damage." |
